Lasagna Recipe

Description

Veg lasagna is a comforting, hearty dish made with layers of tender pasta, a rich tomato sauce, creamy cheese, and a colorful mix of vegetables. It’s satisfying without being heavy and works great for both weeknight dinners and special occasions.

This version swaps meat for sautéed vegetables while keeping all the classic lasagna flavors. The result is a balanced, flavorful dish that even non-vegetarians usually love.

Ingredients

  • Lasagna noodles
  • Olive oil
  • Onion, finely chopped
  • Garlic cloves, minced
  • Zucchini, sliced
  • Bell pepper, chopped
  • Mushrooms, sliced
  • Spinach (fresh or frozen)
  • Crushed tomatoes or tomato sauce
  • Tomato paste
  • Dried oregano
  • Dried basil
  • Salt
  • Black pepper
  • Ricotta cheese
  • Shredded mozzarella cheese
  • Grated Parmesan cheese
  • Fresh basil or parsley (optional, for garnish)

Steps

  1. Preheat the oven to 375°F (190°C).
  2. Cook the lasagna noodles according to package instructions, then drain and set aside.
  3. Heat olive oil in a large pan over medium heat. Add the onion and cook until softened, then add garlic and cook briefly.
  4. Add zucchini, bell pepper, and mushrooms. Cook until the vegetables are tender and lightly browned.
  5. Stir in spinach, crushed tomatoes, tomato paste, oregano, basil, salt, and pepper. Let the sauce simmer for 15–20 minutes.
  6. In a bowl, mix ricotta cheese with Parmesan and the egg (if using).
  7. Spread a thin layer of vegetable sauce in the bottom of a baking dish.
  8. Layer noodles, ricotta mixture, vegetable sauce, and mozzarella cheese. Repeat layers until all ingredients are used, finishing with sauce and mozzarella on top.
  9. Cover with foil and bake for 25 minutes. Remove foil and bake for another 15–20 minutes until bubbly and lightly golden.
  10. Let the lasagna rest for about 10 minutes before slicing and serving.
